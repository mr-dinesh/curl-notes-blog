This was 2013, Srirangapatna, Kaveri Trail Marathon.

The moment I thought I'd pass from the world, survived and went ahead with a player second wind, sprinted towards the finish line — 5hr 40min, just minutes shy of the cutoff.

The Doc who was running along had just saved me. The pits through which I had to crawl and drag myself were now mere memories, dragons slain, a conquest of oneself.

That single span of 30 minutes — heightened agony and pain, followed by a huge rush of endorphins — has been a moment I'll always look back to as the defining *Been There, Done That* moment. The threshold of pain I could endure and go past, and soak in the pleasure on the other side.

---

It's been more than a decade since the sharp pain and the afterglow have both faded. One thing remains: the lesson I understood about myself. The body and mind can and need to be pushed. And the better we are at this skill, the better the payoff, in the long run.

Intuitively, we all understand this. But somehow the drive from within lacks the extra gear and thrust to get us over obstacles which appear larger than they actually are.

I keep thinking of the Doctor, who quietly faded into the background in all of my bragging about having done a full marathon. He appeared magically, helped me cross the mental and physical pain barriers, and went back to his thing.

I spoke to him — Dr. Chandrashekar — briefly, after the run. He was embarrassed at the credit I was trying to give him for literally dragging me from a DNF (Did Not Finish) to a credible first-time finisher. I often reminisce about how much of an impact he had on me — that if it weren't for his gentle intervention, that event would've ended on a totally different note.

---

These angels are always there to step in and help. We need to reach the brink of our pain threshold and then some. They will come, and you will find the missing extra to go ahead.

I find myself heading towards another roadblock, in my work life. Maybe I'll go a little further, and meet a different angel who's been waiting for me.
